# FounderOS Glossary — the jargon-buster

Every term a first-time founder trips over, in one plain sentence, grouped by theme.
Where a FounderOS skill goes deep on a term, it's named in the **Skill** column so you
can jump straight to the method.

> **These definitions are educational, not advice.** They explain the language; they do not
> tell you what's right for your company. Anything legal, tax, or financial (SAFEs, vesting,
> dilution, margins, incorporation) varies by jurisdiction and deal — confirm specifics with
> a qualified local advisor before you sign or rely on them.

---

## 1 — Customers & market

| Term | Plain definition | Skill |
|------|------------------|-------|
| **ICP** (Ideal Customer Profile) | The one specific type of buyer you're built for — "freelance music teachers in the UK," not "everyone." | `validate-idea`, `positioning-and-gtm` |
| **TAM / SAM / SOM** | Total market (everyone who *could* buy), Serviceable market (those you can actually reach/serve), Obtainable market (the slice you can realistically win soon). | `pitch-deck` |
| **Problem statement** | One paragraph, in the customer's own words, naming who has the pain and how badly. | `validate-idea` |
| **PMF** (Product–Market Fit) | The moment people pull the product out of your hands — they'd be genuinely upset if it disappeared. | `positioning-and-gtm`, `scope-mvp` |
| **Positioning** | The one sentence that says who you're for, what you replace, and why you're the obvious choice. | `positioning-and-gtm` |
| **GTM** (Go-To-Market) | Your concrete plan for how the product reaches and converts buyers — channels, message, motion. | `positioning-and-gtm`, `launch-plan` |
| **Moat** | The durable reason a competitor can't just copy you and win (network effects, switching cost, brand, data, regulation). | `pitch-deck` |
| **Traction** | Evidence the market wants this — sign-ups, revenue, usage, retention — not opinions. | `find-first-customers`, `investor-pipeline` |

## 2 — Product & validation

| Term | Plain definition | Skill |
|------|------------------|-------|
| **MVP** (Minimum Viable Product) | The smallest thing you can put in front of real users to learn whether the idea is real — not a tiny version of the dream. | `scope-mvp` |
| **Riskiest assumption** | The single belief that is both most uncertain and most fatal if wrong — test it first. | `validate-idea` |
| **Pivot** | A deliberate change of direction (problem, customer, or model) when the evidence says the current path is dead. | `validate-idea`, `capture-learning` |
| **Concierge / Wizard-of-Oz** | Delivering the service by hand behind the scenes to test demand before you build software. | `scope-mvp` |
| **Mom Test** | An interview technique that gets honest signal by asking about the customer's past behaviour, never your idea. | `customer-interviews` |
| **Cohort** | A group of users grouped by when they started, tracked over time to see if behaviour improves. | `runway-and-unit-economics` |
| **North-star metric** | The single number that best captures the value customers get — the one you steer by. | `runway-and-unit-economics`, `capture-learning` |
| **KPI** (Key Performance Indicator) | Any measurable number you track to know if you're winning; pick few, pick honest. | `runway-and-unit-economics` |

## 3 — Launch & growth

| Term | Plain definition | Skill |
|------|------------------|-------|
| **Cold outreach** | Contacting people who don't know you yet (email, DM, call) to start a buying conversation. | `find-first-customers` |
| **Channel** | A specific repeatable path to reach buyers (e.g. cold email, SEO, a partner, a marketplace). | `positioning-and-gtm`, `launch-plan` |
| **Conversion** | The share of people who take the next step you wanted (visit → sign-up → pay). | `launch-plan`, `find-first-customers` |
| **Retention** | The share of users who keep coming back — usually the truest sign of value. | `runway-and-unit-economics` |
| **Churn** | The share of customers (or revenue) you lose in a period; high churn quietly kills growth. | `runway-and-unit-economics` |

## 4 — Money & unit economics

| Term | Plain definition | Skill |
|------|------------------|-------|
| **Burn** | The cash you spend per month beyond what you bring in (net burn = out minus in). | `runway-and-unit-economics` |
| **Runway** | How many months of cash you have left at current burn before you hit zero. | `runway-and-unit-economics` |
| **Bootstrapping** | Funding the company from revenue and savings instead of investors — you keep control, you grow slower. | `runway-and-unit-economics` |
| **MRR / ARR** | Monthly / Annual Recurring Revenue — predictable subscription revenue, the unit investors size you by. | `runway-and-unit-economics` |
| **CAC** (Customer Acquisition Cost) | The fully-loaded cost to win one paying customer (sales + marketing ÷ customers won). | `runway-and-unit-economics`, `find-first-customers` |
| **LTV** (Lifetime Value) | The total profit you expect from one customer before they churn. | `runway-and-unit-economics` |
| **LTV:CAC** | The ratio of what a customer is worth to what they cost to win; a common rule of thumb is roughly 3:1, but it varies. | `runway-and-unit-economics` |
| **Payback period** | How many months of a customer's payments it takes to earn back their CAC. | `runway-and-unit-economics` |
| **Gross margin** | Revenue minus the direct cost of delivering it, as a percentage — how much each sale actually keeps. | `runway-and-unit-economics` |
| **Contribution margin** | What one extra sale adds after its variable costs — what's left to cover fixed costs and profit. | `runway-and-unit-economics` |
| **Unit economics** | Whether a single customer (the "unit") makes or loses money once you account for cost to serve and acquire. | `runway-and-unit-economics` |

## 5 — Fundraising & cap table

| Term | Plain definition | Skill |
|------|------------------|-------|
| **Angel** | An individual who invests their own money early, often the first cheque in. | `investor-pipeline` |
| **Lead investor** | The investor who sets the terms and price for a round; others follow them. | `investor-pipeline` |
| **Accelerator** | A fixed-term programme that gives money, mentorship, and a network, usually for equity. | `investor-pipeline` |
| **SAFE** | "Simple Agreement for Future Equity" — money now that converts to shares later, without setting a price today. | `investor-pipeline` |
| **Convertible note** | Like a SAFE but structured as debt that converts to equity later, often with interest and a maturity date. | `investor-pipeline` |
| **Equity** | Ownership in the company, expressed as shares or a percentage. | `cofounder-and-equity` |
| **Dilution** | The shrinking of your ownership percentage when new shares are issued (e.g. to investors or employees). | `cofounder-and-equity`, `investor-pipeline` |
| **Cap table** | The list of who owns what — every shareholder, option, and instrument. | `cofounder-and-equity` |
| **Vesting** | Earning your equity over time (commonly four years) rather than getting it all on day one. | `cofounder-and-equity` |
| **Cliff** | The minimum time you must stay before *any* equity vests (commonly one year); leave earlier and you get nothing. | `cofounder-and-equity` |
| **Pre-money / post-money** | A company's value *before* new investment (pre) and *after* it (post = pre + amount raised). | `investor-pipeline`, `pitch-deck` |
| **Term sheet** | The short, mostly non-binding document outlining the key terms of an investment before legal drafting. | `investor-pipeline` |
| **Due diligence** | The investor's deep check of your numbers, legals, and claims before they wire money. | `investor-pipeline` |
| **Data room** | The organised, shareable folder of documents investors review during due diligence. | `investor-pipeline` |

## 6 — Team & company

| Term | Plain definition | Skill |
|------|------------------|-------|
| **Co-founder** | A partner who shares ownership, risk, and the earliest work — choose carefully, it's hard to undo. | `cofounder-and-equity` |
| **First hire** | Your first non-founder employee; the role you hire defines what the company becomes. | `first-hire` |
| **Incorporation** | Legally forming the company as a distinct entity (the type and steps vary by country). | `legal-and-incorporation` |
| **Runway (decision sense)** | Used loosely to mean "time to figure it out before the money runs out" — drives nearly every founder trade-off. | `runway-and-unit-economics` |
