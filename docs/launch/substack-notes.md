# Substack Notes - FounderOS launch pack

Short, feed-native Notes for Substack. Notes reward a single sharp idea + a soft pull, not a
hard sell. Post one every day or two around launch; mix them with replies to others. Keep the irony: *proof before hype* shouldn't be hyped.

---

**Note 1 - the announcement**
I just open-sourced FounderOS: the operating system I wish I'd had as a first-time founder.
22 AI skills covering the whole journey - idea → validation → MVP → launch → fundraising →
operations. Free, attribution-only. One rule, from 25 years in the lab: proof before hype.
→ https://github.com/impactbrussels/FounderOS

**Note 2 - the origin**
For 25 years I demanded evidence before excitement in food & biotech R&D. Then I taught myself
to build with AI and became the first-time founder I used to advise. Same wall everyone hits:
not *how* to do a thing, but *which* thing next. So I built the map. It's open now. https://github.com/impactbrussels/FounderOS

**Note 3 - the belief problem (ties to the book)**
You can have great science and still lose the market. Most founders don't have a data problem -
they have a *belief* problem. Buyers and investors can't see what the work unlocks in real life.
That insight runs through my book *Proof Before Hype* - and through the open OS I built from it.

**Note 4 - give value (a tactic)**
Free founder tactic: before you build anything, write down the one assumption that, if wrong,
kills the idea. Then design the cheapest test that could prove it wrong - and set the pass/fail
number *before* you run it. That's the whole `validate-idea` skill in FounderOS. https://github.com/impactbrussels/FounderOS

**Note 5 - first vs fifth purchase**
Trial and repeat are different problems. Curiosity gets the first purchase (cheap). Belief gets
the fifth (expensive). Most startups design only for the first and wonder why nobody comes back.
Design for both, separately. (One of the frameworks baked into FounderOS.) https://github.com/impactbrussels/FounderOS

**Note 6 - anti-gatekeeping**
Startup know-how is gatekept and sold as $5k courses. I don't think it should be. FounderOS is
the entire operating system - open, free, attribution-only. Take it, use it, build something.
Sponsorship keeps it free; it never buys influence over the advice. https://github.com/impactbrussels/FounderOS

**Note 7 - for non-technical founders**
You don't need to code to use FounderOS. Every skill has a copy-paste prompt you drop into
ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini, fill in your details, and go. Zero setup. If you can paste, you can
run the whole founder playbook. https://github.com/impactbrussels/FounderOS

**Note 8 - call for contributors**
FounderOS ships with 22 skills and a roadmap of ~85 more - each one a "good first issue." If
you've built or advised a startup, come teach the next founder how. Open repo, open roadmap,
attribution guaranteed. https://github.com/impactbrussels/FounderOS

**Note 9 - the quiet flex (proof, not hype)**
No launch hype here. Just a working thing: 22 skills, runnable in Claude Code / Codex / Cursor
or any chatbot, grounded in real frameworks and cited sources. Try one, tell me where it's
wrong. That's the whole pitch. https://github.com/impactbrussels/FounderOS

## How to use these
- **Cadence:** 1 note every 1-2 days for ~2 weeks. Lead with Note 1 on launch day.
- **Pair with the long post:** when your FoodEdge Substack launch essay goes out, drop Note 3 or
  Note 6 as the Note that links to it.
- **Engage, don't broadcast:** reply to other founders' notes with a relevant FounderOS tactic
  (Notes 4/5) rather than always posting your own.
- **One link per note**, soft CTA. Let curiosity pull.
