# Launch Checklist & Templates (reference)

Frameworks behind the `launch-plan` skill. Theme-agnostic; the founder supplies the domain.

## Launch-type menu

| Type | What it is | Pick it when | Primary `[METRIC]` | Main prep |
|------|-----------|--------------|--------------------|-----------|
| **Quiet beta** | Hand-pick a handful of users, ship privately | Product is raw; you need feedback, not noise | Activated users / retention | Onboarding by hand, a feedback loop |
| **Waitlist** | Promote the promise, collect emails before GA | You want demand signal before building more | Sign-ups + sign-up rate | Landing page, referral hook |
| **Public launch** | Coordinated "we're live" to an audience you have | You've warmed a list/following and have proof | Sign-ups / sales on the day | Warm list, launch copy, supporters |
| **Platform launch** | List on a directory, marketplace, app store, or Product-Hunt-style site | Buyers already gather there with intent | Ranking / installs / leads | Listing assets, day-one mobilisation, reviews |

You can stack them in sequence (waitlist → beta → public). Don't run a **public** launch with a
cold audience - that's launching into a void.

## Asset checklist

Tick only what the chosen launch type needs. More assets ≠ better launch.

- [ ] **Landing page** - headline = positioning statement, one clear CTA tied to `[METRIC]`
- [ ] **Demo or video** - 30-90s showing the value, not the menu (skip for quiet beta)
- [ ] **Launch copy** - the post/thread, the email, the DM script (all inherit the messaging hierarchy)
- [ ] **FAQ** - pre-empts the top 5 objections (price, security, "is this for me?", migration, support)
- [ ] **Warm outreach list** - supporters, early users, communities you'll personally notify
- [ ] **Tracking** - analytics/UTM so you can read `[METRIC]` in real time, not guess
- [ ] **Support plan** - who answers questions on launch day, and how fast
- [ ] **Visuals** - screenshots/GIF/social card (platform launches live or die on the thumbnail)
- [ ] **Press/founder note** - only if a public or platform launch warrants it

## T-minus timeline template

Adjust the spacing to your runway; the *order* is what matters.

| When | Phase | Actions |
|------|-------|---------|
| **T-14 to T-7** | Pre | Lock launch type + `[METRIC]` bar. Build assets. Start warming the list (teasers, behind-the-scenes). Line up supporters/amplifiers and confirm them. |
| **T-3 to T-1** | Pre | Freeze and proofread all copy. Dry-run the product flow end to end. Schedule posts/emails. Brief everyone on the run of show. Prep the FAQ + support coverage. |
| **T-0 morning** | During | Go live early. Post in priority order. Personally notify the warm list. Watch `[METRIC]` and the funnel in real time. |
| **T-0 all day** | During | Respond to every comment/DM fast. Amplifiers share on cue. Fix anything broken immediately. Thank early adopters publicly. |
| **T+1 to T+3** | Post | Follow up with everyone who engaged but didn't convert. Send a "thank you / here's what's next" note. Convert attention into `[METRIC]`. |
| **T+7** | Post | Run the post-launch review. Decide the next launch (new channel / segment / feature). |

## Post-launch review template

> **Goal & bar:** we aimed for `[METRIC]` ≥ [number].
> **Result:** we hit [actual]. Verdict: **hit / missed / mixed**.
> **What worked:** [the channel, message, or moment that drove most of the result].
> **What didn't:** [the asset, timing, or channel that underdelivered, and why].
> **Surprise:** [the thing we didn't predict - often the most valuable learning].
> **Next launch:** [what we'll launch next - new segment / channel / feature - and the one change we'll make].

## Anti-patterns

- **Launching to no audience** - a public launch with zero warm list. Attention doesn't self-assemble; warm the list in the pre-phase.
- **One-shot thinking** - treating launch day as the whole strategy. Real growth comes from the post-phase and repeated launches.
- **Asset bloat** - building a press kit and three videos for a 10-person beta. Match assets to the launch type.
- **Vanity metric** - celebrating upvotes/traffic that never convert to the goal. Pick a `[METRIC]` that maps to real outcomes.
- **No run of show** - improvising on launch day. Sequence who posts what, when, and who responds.
- **Silent post-phase** - letting interested people drift away because nobody followed up within 72 hours.
- **Launching on a Friday/holiday** - when your `[ICP]` and amplifiers are offline. Mind the calendar.

## Further reading (verify against primary sources)

*Obviously Awesome* (April Dunford, for the launch message) · the Product Hunt / Indie Hackers
launch playbooks · *The Mom Test* (for the beta feedback loop) · *Traction* (Weinberg & Mares,
for which channel to launch on). Treat these as method sources; your `[ICP]`'s response on the day
beats any checklist.
