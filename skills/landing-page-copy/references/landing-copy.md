# Landing Copy Reference

Deeper frameworks behind the `landing-page-copy` skill. Theme-agnostic; the founder supplies the
domain and the real proof. Examples are *structural illustrations* - never copy them; never
fabricate the numbers in them.

## The one rule: one page, one job

A landing page is not a website. It exists to move **[ICP]** toward **one** next action. Every
section earns its place by serving that job; anything that doesn't, cut it. Multiple goals = no goal.

## Section-by-section template (top to bottom)

| # | Section | Job | What goes in it |
|---|---------|-----|-----------------|
| 1 | **Headline + subhead** | Stop the scroll; say what/who/outcome | One sharp claim + one clarifying line |
| 2 | **Problem** | Make [ICP] feel understood | Name [PROBLEM] in their words; agitate just enough |
| 3 | **Solution / value** | Show the after-state | The outcome; features only as *evidence* for it |
| 4 | **How it works** | Make it feel achievable | 3 simple steps, plain verbs |
| 5 | **Proof** | Replace claims with evidence | Testimonials, logos, numbers, before/after |
| 6 | **Objection / FAQ** | Remove the last blocker | Answer the #1 hesitation directly |
| 7 | **Single CTA** | Convert | One action, in their words, repeated |

The page should read as a logical argument: *here's your problem → here's the outcome → here's how
→ here's proof it works → here's why your worry is handled → do this one thing.*

## Headline formulas (with good vs. bad)

| Formula | Good (clear, outcome-led) | Bad (vague / feature-led / hype) |
|---------|---------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Outcome + for whom | "Close your books in a day - for solo accountants" | "The future of accounting" |
| Problem → relief | "Stop chasing invoices. Get paid on time." | "Powerful invoicing platform" |
| [Do X] without [pain] | "Ship your site without touching code" | "Synergistic web solutions" |
| Specific result | "Cut onboarding from 2 weeks to 2 days" | "Streamline your workflow" |

Write 5-10. Read each cold: does a stranger know what it is, who it's for, and the payoff? Keep the
clearest. **Clever that needs decoding loses to clear that lands instantly.**

## Features → benefits (translate before you publish)

| Feature (what it is) | Benefit (so you can…) - this is what goes on the page |
|----------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| "Real-time sync" | "Never lose work or wonder which version is current" |
| "AI-powered tagging" | "Find any file in seconds instead of scrolling for ten minutes" |
| "256-bit encryption" | "Sleep at night knowing client data is safe" |

Rule: if you can't finish "…so you can ___," the feature isn't ready for the page.

## Proof ladder (use the strongest you honestly have)

| Strength | Form | Note |
|----------|------|------|
| Strongest | Named customer results with numbers | Specific + attributed + quantified |
| Strong | Testimonials with names/photos/roles | Real people, real quotes |
| Strong | Recognisable logos / counts ("1,200 teams") | Only if true |
| Medium | Before/after, demo, case detail | Show the transformation |
| Honest fallback | Founder credibility, clear demo, risk-reversal/guarantee | Use when you have no customers *yet* - then earn the rest |

**Never fabricate.** Fake logos, invented stats, and made-up testimonials destroy trust and can be
illegal. No proof yet? Use the honest fallback and flag it to upgrade later.

## Voice / [TONE] guidance

- Write at the reading level of a busy, distracted `[ICP]` - short words, short sentences.
- Match `[TONE]` to the audience and stakes (calm/clinical for risk; warm/punchy for consumer).
- Use "you" far more than "we." The page is about them.
- Cut every word that doesn't add meaning. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it.

## Conversion anti-patterns

- **Feature-dumping** - listing capabilities instead of outcomes the visitor cares about.
- **Multiple CTAs** - "Sign up / Book demo / Read blog / Follow us" splits attention; pick one.
- **Jargon & buzzwords** - "synergistic, robust, next-gen, leverage." Say it plainly or cut it.
- **Hype without proof** - "revolutionary," "game-changing" with nothing behind it.
- **Burying the lead** - making the visitor scroll to learn what you even do.
- **Talking about yourself** - company history above the fold; nobody cares yet.
- **Fabricated proof** - fake numbers/logos/testimonials. Never.

Joanna Wiebe / Copyhackers on conversion copy · the "features vs. benefits" classic.

(test headlines and CTAs; don't assume).

**Frameworks from the author's books (generalized):**
