# Consumer Product Testing Playbook (reference)

Choosing and running the right product test by stage. Theme-agnostic across food, beverage,
personal care, cosmetics, household, hardware, and packaged goods. Confirm **safety, consent,
allergen/labelling** with a qualified expert before testing anything ingested or applied.

## The test menu by stage

| Stage | Test | What it is | Good signal |
|-------|------|-----------|-------------|
| Concept | **Concept test** | Show a description / mockup / pack; ask intent + price reaction | Strangers in the [ICP] show real intent, not politeness |
| Concept | **Fake-door / pre-order** | A "buy"/"reserve" button before the product exists | Real reservations or pre-payment above your bar |
| Prototype | **Functional / usability test** | Watch target users actually use the prototype | They complete the core task unaided and want to repeat |
| Prototype | **Sensory test** (→ `sensory-testing`) | Blind, benchmarked taste/smell/feel test | Liked ≥ benchmark; or no perceptible downgrade |
| Pre-launch | **Home-Use Test (HUT)** | Users use it at home over days/weeks | Sustained use, re-use intent, few drop-offs |
| Pre-launch | **Central Location Test (CLT)** | Controlled venue, many users, structured | Stable, controlled read on performance/liking |
| Claims/shelf | **Claims substantiation** | Evidence a marketing claim is true and lawful | Defensible data; verify legal requirements |
| Claims/shelf | **Shelf / pack A/B** | Which pack/price gets picked in a realistic choice | Higher pick-rate at target price |

## Recruiting & sample size

- **Screen for the real [ICP].** Use a short screener; reject out-of-target and friends/family.
- **Qualitative usability:** ~5–8 users per round surfaces most usability problems; iterate.
- **Quantitative (liking, preference, claims):** target-consumer samples in the tens-to-hundreds;
  treat small panels as directional. (For sensory specifics, see `sensory-testing`.)
- **Incentivise fairly** without biasing (a thank-you, not a reward contingent on praise).

## Observation sheet (what to record)

- Did they complete the core use **unaided**? Where did they hesitate, misuse, or quit?
- Would they use it **again** — and did they, unprompted, on a later day (the real test)?
- Would they **pay** / re-order? At what price?
- Spontaneous comments (verbatim) vs prompted ratings — weight the spontaneous.

## Setting the bar (before you test)

> "If ≥ [X of N] complete the core use unaided AND ≥ [Y] would re-use/re-order (and, if
> sensory, liking ≥ benchmark) → proceed. Else reformulate/redesign and re-test."

## Anti-patterns

- Testing with friends/family or a generic crowd instead of the screened [ICP].
- Asking opinions instead of observing behaviour.
- Wrong test for the stage (home-use test on a concept; concept test on a finished product).
- Single round, no iteration; or moving the bar after a weak result.
- Skipping a blind sensory benchmark for tasted/smelled/applied products.
- Ignoring safety, consent, allergens, or labelling rules.

## Further reading (verify against primary sources)

Lawless & Heymann, *Sensory Evaluation of Food*; Nielsen Norman Group on usability test sample
sizes (the "5 users" guidance and its limits); ISO 11136 (consumer acceptance); ESOMAR guidance
on research ethics/consent. Your evidence about *your* users beats any rule of thumb.
