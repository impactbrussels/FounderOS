# Launch Flow

Closes the most common gap in a first build: "it works" and "anyone is paying for it" are
different events, often months apart. This flow turns a finished MVP into first paying customers
by getting the message right *before* the launch motion, and the launch motion right *before*
chasing volume. Order matters - a launch with fuzzy positioning just burns your warmest leads.

Each step ends at a CHECKPOINT where the founder reviews the draft and commits. The flow drafts;
the founder decides what ships.

## The sequence

### Step 1 - Sharpen the message (skill: `positioning-and-gtm`)

Run `positioning-and-gtm` for the `[ICP]`, `[PROBLEM]`, and `[ONE_LINER]`.

Produces:
- A positioning statement: for whom, against what alternative, the one wedge you win on.
- The single primary `[CHANNEL]` to go to market on (not five - one).
- A value proposition and the proof you can actually show today.

**CHECKPOINT 1 - Can a stranger repeat your pitch back correctly?**
The founder pressure-tests the positioning against a real prospect or the `customer-proxy` agent.
If the wedge is generic ("better, faster, cheaper") or the channel is "all of them," fix it before
building any launch assets. Do not proceed on vague positioning.

### Step 2 - Build the launch motion (skill: `launch-plan`)

Run `launch-plan` using the positioning and channel from Step 1.

Produces:
- A dated launch sequence (pre-launch list, launch day, follow-through), not a single big-bang.
- Assets and copy mapped to the chosen `[CHANNEL]`.
- A launch `[GOAL]` and the `[METRIC]` that defines success (e.g. demos booked, sign-ups, sales).

**CHECKPOINT 2 - Is the goal a real outcome, not a vanity spike?**
The founder reviews: does the launch goal point at revenue or qualified pipeline, not just traffic
or likes? Is there a pre-launch audience to launch *to*? If the launch list is empty, pause and
build it first - a launch to nobody is a rehearsal, not a launch.

### Step 3 - Convert the first customers (skill: `find-first-customers`)

Run `find-first-customers` to run direct outreach and close the earliest deals.

Produces:
- A target list of named, reachable prospects in the `[ICP]`.
- An outreach sequence and a simple qualifying + close motion (founder-led sales).
- A pipeline tracker: contacted → replied → call → closed, with conversion at each stage.

**CHECKPOINT 3 - Are real people paying, or just nodding?**
The founder reviews actual conversions against the Step 2 metric. "Interested" is not a customer;
a signed order or a payment is. If outreach replies are strong but nobody closes, the gap is
usually positioning or price - loop back to Step 1, don't just send more emails.

## What the flow outputs

A live go-to-market: locked positioning, an executed launch with measured results, an active
outreach pipeline, and - the only outcome that counts - your **first paying customers** with the
conversion data behind them. After the launch produces real numbers, run `capture-learning` to
record what the channel and message actually did, so the next launch starts smarter.
