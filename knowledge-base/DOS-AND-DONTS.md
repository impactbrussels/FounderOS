# FounderOS Dos & Don'ts

Concrete operating rules for using FounderOS and AI well as a founder. Grown by the
`capture-learning` skill — append under the right heading, never overwrite.

## DO

- **DO replace every placeholder with real specifics** — `[ICP]`, `[PROBLEM]`, `[CONSTRAINT]`. Generic input produces generic output. *(seed)*
- **DO pick the lightest prompt tier that works** — Low for quick one-shots, High only for decision-grade work. *(seed)*
- **DO state the pass/fail bar before running any experiment** — it's what stops you rationalising a dead idea. *(seed)*
- **DO route a lost founder to ONE next step**, not a syllabus. Overwhelm is the enemy. *(seed)*
- **DO source numbers** (market size, benchmarks) from real data and cite them. *(seed)*
- **DO batch changes** — generate, gather all feedback, revise once; don't churn after every micro-edit. *(seed)*
- **DO confirm legal/tax/equity specifics with a qualified local professional** before acting. *(seed)*
- **DO capture a lesson the moment a real outcome lands** — use `capture-learning` while it's fresh. *(seed)*

## DON'T

- **DON'T validate with friends and family** — they protect your feelings, not your runway. *(seed)*
- **DON'T build before confirming the problem is real and painful.** *(seed)*
- **DON'T run an experiment you can't fail** — if every outcome means "keep going," it's theatre. *(seed)*
- **DON'T paste secrets, credentials, or customer PII into any prompt.** Redact first. *(seed)*
- **DON'T invent facts to sound confident** — say "I don't know yet, here's how to find out." *(seed)*
- **DON'T state a specific legal threshold or tax rate as fact** — rules vary by jurisdiction; tell the founder to verify. *(seed)*
- **DON'T split equity on a handshake or skip vesting** — it's the classic founder catastrophe. *(seed)*
- **DON'T chase every channel at once** — pick one or two where the ICP actually is. *(seed)*
