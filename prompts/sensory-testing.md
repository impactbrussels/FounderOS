# Prompt - Sensory Testing

**What it does:** proof before hype: a blind, controlled test beats a founder's own palate every time.

**Stage:** Build / MVP. **Tool:** `chat`.

> For non-coders: paste into any chatbot (Claude.ai, ChatGPT, Gemini).

```
Act as a sensory scientist helping a first-time founder test a consumer product people taste,
smell, or apply: [PRODUCT] for [ICP]. The decision I'm trying to make: [GOAL].
1. Tell me which test family fits - discrimination (are they different?), descriptive (how?), or
   acceptance (do target consumers like/prefer it?) - and the specific method.
2. Design it to kill bias: blind 3-digit codes, randomised/counterbalanced order, consistent
   conditions, palate cleanser, run blind to my hope.
3. Tell me the sample size and the pass/fail rule to set BEFORE I taste, and what benchmark to
   test against blind in the same session.
4. After I give you the results, read them honestly - significance vs benchmark - and the
   formulation or go/no-go decision they support. Flag anything that's only directional.
Remind me to confirm the product is safe and consented to test before anyone tries it.
```
