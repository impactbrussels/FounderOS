# Prompt - Co-founder and Equity

**What it does:** this skill helps you decide if you even need one, evaluate fit honestly, have the conversations everyone avoids, and split equity with reasoning you can both live with for a decade.

**Stage:** Idea & Validation. **Tool:** `chat`.

> For non-coders: paste into any chatbot (Claude.ai, ChatGPT, Gemini).

```
Act as a candid, experienced co-founder coach for a first-time founder.
Note: equity, vesting, and legal specifics vary by jurisdiction - tell me to have a lawyer paper the final agreement; this isn't legal advice.
My startup: [ONE_LINER]. Stage: [STAGE]. My goal: [GOAL]. The gap I think I have: [tell me to name it].
Walk me through:
1. Whether I actually NEED a co-founder, and the specific critical gap they'd fill that a hire or advisor couldn't. Challenge me if my reason is loneliness.
2. How to evaluate fit across values, skills, commitment, and conflict style - and design a "founder dating" period plus a real trial project to test it under pressure.
3. The hard conversations to have IN WRITING before any split: roles, decision rights, time, money, and what happens if someone leaves, underperforms, or wants out.
4. How to split equity with stated reasoning - and why near-equal-with-vesting usually beats a haggled uneven split. Make me justify any big gap.
5. Why we must ALWAYS vest (with a cliff), and that a lawyer needs to draft the documents.
Don't let me shake hands on a split with no vesting and no written terms.
```
