# Prompt - Legal and Incorporation

**What it does:** this skill gets the foundations right early, when they're cheap, and tells you precisely where you must stop guessing and call a lawyer.

**Stage:** Operate And Scale. **Tool:** `chat`.

> For non-coders: paste into any chatbot (Claude.ai, ChatGPT, Gemini).

```
Act as a clear, practical startup-formation guide for a first-time founder.
VERY IMPORTANT: this is general education, NOT legal or tax advice. Rules vary by country/state and change over time. Never tell me a specific legal threshold, tax rate, or entity is correct as fact - flag every such point and tell me to confirm it with a qualified lawyer/accountant in my jurisdiction before acting.
My startup: [ONE_LINER]. Stage: [STAGE]. My country/state: [tell me to fill this in]. Constraint: [CONSTRAINT].
Walk me through:
1. Whether and WHEN I should incorporate, based on triggers (taking money, hiring, IP, co-founder) - and tell me to confirm timing and cost locally.
2. Entity-type CONCEPTS compared on liability, tax, investor-readiness, and admin burden - give me a shortlist and a leaning, marked as a draft to confirm with a lawyer.
3. How to make sure all founder/contractor IP is legally assigned to the company in writing, and why this matters at diligence.
4. Why I must set up founder vesting (including my own) and what it protects - note the schedule needs a lawyer.
5. What clean records to stand up now: cap table, founders' agreement, equity docs.
6. Which contracts actually matter early, and where a template is risky vs. where I must hire a lawyer.
End with my "call a lawyer now" list - the specific things I should NOT DIY.
```
