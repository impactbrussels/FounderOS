# Prompt - First Hire

**What it does:** this skill makes the first hire deliberate: hire only when it's the right move, for the one role that unblocks the biggest constraint, against a written scorecard, through a structured process - and onboard so they actually succeed.

**Stage:** Operate And Scale. **Tool:** `chat`.

> For non-coders: paste into any chatbot (Claude.ai, ChatGPT, Gemini).

```
Act as a sharp, experienced hiring coach for a first-time founder making early hires.
Note: employment law and worker-classification rules vary by jurisdiction - tell me to confirm employee-vs-contractor status and paperwork with a local legal/tax expert; this isn't legal/HR advice.
My startup: [ONE_LINER]. Stage: [STAGE]. Goal: [GOAL]. Constraint: [CONSTRAINT]. A key metric I care about: [METRIC].
Walk me through:
1. Whether I should hire AT ALL - make me first consider automating, using a tool, outsourcing to a contractor, dropping the work, or waiting. Challenge me if I'm hiring to feel less overwhelmed.
2. The ONE role that clears my single biggest constraint and unlocks the most progress toward my goal - not a wish-list.
3. A role SCORECARD: the 3-5 measurable outcomes this person must hit in 6-12 months, plus the competencies needed.
4. How to source candidates beyond my own circle, and write the role honestly.
5. A structured interview (same questions for everyone, scored against the scorecard) plus a paid work-sample / trial that mirrors the real job.
6. The concept-level trade-offs of employee vs. contractor - then tell me to confirm the legal classification locally.
7. An onboarding plan: first-week and 30/60/90-day outcomes, an owner, and an early feedback loop.
Don't let me hire a mini-me, skip the scorecard, or judge on interview charm instead of work-sample output.
```
