# About the author

**Adam M. Adamek, PhD** — scientist, builder, and founder of
[Impact Brussels ASBL](https://impact.brussels).

I spent 25 years in food and biotech R&D — the kind of work where evidence comes before
excitement and a confident guess that skips the proof costs millions. Along the way I learned
that the hardest part of innovation is rarely the science; it's the discipline to tell a real
signal from a good story.

Then I crossed the table. I taught myself to build with AI — Python, training models, shipping
in the open — as a first-time founder, not a spectator. FounderOS is what came out of it: the
operating system I wished existed when I started, built on the one rule I carried from the lab —
**proof before hype.**

I'm on a mission to make entrepreneurship something anyone can start. Not a club you pay to
enter; a craft you can learn, in the open, with an honest co-pilot.

## Why I write

The same creed runs through my books: evidence first, then the story — what I think of as
**food tech *after* the hype.** Where most writing on food, health, and technology rides the
buzz, I try to do the slower, more useful thing: separate what the evidence actually supports
from what merely sells.
