# Customer interview guide - the Mom Test (fill-in)

> The Mom Test: ask questions even your mum couldn't lie to you on. Talk about the customer's
> **past behaviour and real life**, never your idea. The moment you pitch, you start collecting
> compliments instead of truth. Fill `[PLACEHOLDERS]`, run 5-10 of these before building anything.
> Pairs with `customer-interviews` and `validate-idea`.

**Who I'm interviewing (`[ICP]`):** ____  ·  **The pain I'm exploring (`[PROBLEM]`):** ____
**Date / name (or code):** ____  ·  **Goal of this interview:** learn, not sell.

---

## 1. Intro (30 seconds - set them at ease)
"Thanks for the time. I'm trying to understand how `[ICP]` handle `[the area, NOT your solution]`
today. There are no right answers and I'm not selling anything - I just want to learn how you
actually do it. Mind if I take notes?"

> Do **not** describe your idea. Not yet. Maybe not at all.

## 2. Their world & the real pain (past behaviour, not hypotheticals)
- "Walk me through the last time you `[did the relevant task]`. What happened, step by step?"
- "How often does that come up?"
- "What's the most frustrating part of it?"
- "What did you do *last time* it went wrong? What did that cost you (time / money / stress)?"
- "Have you tried to fix this before? What did you try, and why did you stop?"
- "How are you handling it right now?" *(the real competitor - spreadsheet, workaround, or nothing)*

> Listen for: specific stories, recent events, real money/time, things they've already tried to
> solve. Vague hypotheticals ("yeah I'd probably use that") are not signal.

## 3. Probe the cost (is it a paid problem?)
- "When this happens, how much does it actually cost you?"
- "Is fixing it something you've spent money or time on already? On what?"
- "If you could wave a wand, what would change - and how much would that be worth to you?"

## 4. Commitment asks (the truth test - do they give something real?)
Talk is cheap; commitment isn't. Ask for one of these - currency, time, or reputation:
- **Time:** "Would you be up for a 20-minute follow-up to look at something I'm working on?"
- **Reputation:** "Who else struggles with this that I should talk to? Could you intro me?"
- **Money / intent:** "If a tool fixed exactly this for `[price]`/month, would you put your card down to try it?" *(Watch what they* do*, not what they say.)*

> A "yes, sounds great!" with no commitment behind it is a polite no. Record the *commitment*, not the compliment.

## 5. Wrap
"This was hugely helpful. Can I come back to you as I learn more?" Then stop talking and listen.
