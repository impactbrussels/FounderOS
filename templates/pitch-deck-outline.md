# Pitch deck outline - fill-in (10-12 slides)

> One job per slide. If a slide does two jobs, split it; if it does none, cut it. Fill the
> `[PLACEHOLDERS]`, delete the guidance once written. Pairs with the `pitch-deck` and
> `investor-pipeline` skills. Numbers must be real or labelled as estimates - never invent
> market sizes or financials.

---

**Slide 1 - Title / hook**
`[STARTUP_NAME]` - `[ONE_LINER]` (one sentence a stranger understands).
Founder name(s), stage, one contact line.
*Job: make them want the next slide.*

**Slide 2 - Problem**
Who (`[ICP]`) has what painful job (`[PROBLEM]`), how often, and how they cope today.
One vivid, specific example beats three abstractions.
*Job: make the pain feel real and expensive.*

**Slide 3 - Solution**
What you built and the single "aha" it delivers. Show, don't list features.
*Job: the one-line "oh, that's clever."*

**Slide 4 - How it works**
The 3-step flow from customer's point of view (do X → Loop does Y → they get Z).
*Job: prove it's real and simple.*

**Slide 5 - Market (TAM / SAM / SOM)**
Total / serviceable / obtainable. Show your math and **cite the source**. Bottom-up beats top-down.
*Job: show the prize is big enough - honestly.*

**Slide 6 - Traction**
The strongest evidence you have: revenue, users, retention, signed pilots, waitlist. A chart up and to the right.
*Job: prove the market is already pulling.*

**Slide 7 - Business model**
How you make money: price, `[METRIC]` you grow, gross margin. Keep it to one screen.
*Job: show each customer makes money.*

**Slide 8 - Go-to-market**
The one `[CHANNEL]` that's working (or your best evidence-backed bet) and why it scales.
*Job: prove you can reach buyers repeatably and affordably.*

**Slide 9 - Competition / why you win**
The honest landscape (incl. "a spreadsheet" / "doing nothing") and your `[moat]` - why you stay ahead.
*Job: show you're not naive about rivals.*

**Slide 10 - Team**
Why *this* team wins *this* problem. Unfair advantages, relevant scars. Roles, not résumés.
*Job: make them bet on the jockeys.*

**Slide 11 - The ask**
How much you're raising, `[runway]` it buys, the 3-4 milestones it hits, and the instrument (e.g. SAFE).
*Job: make the next step concrete.*

**Slide 12 - Vision / appendix (optional)**
Where this goes if it works - one credible, non-hype sentence. Park deep financials in an appendix.
*Job: leave them imagining the upside.*

---

### Quick rules
- 10-12 slides, one idea each, readable from the back of a room.
- Every number sourced or labelled an estimate.
- Tell a story across slides 2→6: pain → fix → it works → people want it.
- A draft deck is not a strategy - pressure-test it with `investor-pipeline` (or the skeptical-investor agent) before sending.
